<li><p>Determine which of the following are statements. If not, explain
why not. If so, determine the truth value of the statement.</p>
<ol type="a">
<li><p>Calvin Coolidge was the greatest American president.</p></li>
<li><p>The square root of a rational number is always a rational number.</p></li>
<li><p>When will the Philadelphia Phillies win the World Series?</p></li>
<li><p>This sentence is false.</p></li>
<li><p><span class="math inline"><em>n</em>(<em>n</em> + 1)/2</span>.</p></li>
</ol></li>
</ol>

<Spoiler>
a. Yes, but it is false; Calvin Coolidge is the Silent President.

b. Yes, but it is false; $\sqrt{2}$ is a clear disproof.

c. No, it is a question.

d. No, it is paradoxical.

e. No, cannot be true or false.
</Spoiler>
